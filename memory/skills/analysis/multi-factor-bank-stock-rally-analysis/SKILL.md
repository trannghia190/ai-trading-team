# Workflow: Multi-Factor Analysis of a Bank Stock Price Rally

## 1. Macro Context
- Evaluate interest rates, monetary policy, banking-system risk, FX conditions, and the direction of large-scale market liquidity.
- Note the sector-wide support factors for banks in general (for example: low rates, credit-growth improvement, supportive SBV policy).

## 2. Money-Flow and Trading Behavior
- Analyze net buying/selling behavior across institutions, domestic individuals, and foreign investors.
- Track trading volume, participation from large capital, FOMO behavior, and foreign-ownership-room effects.

## 3. Earnings Results and Company News
- Compare the latest business results versus the prior quarter / same period, focusing on net profit, NIM, provisioning expense, and asset quality.
- Evaluate the impact of special events such as restructuring, M&A, major-ownership rumors, private placements, and dividend actions.

## 4. Technical Analysis
- Identify the main price structure (breakout, rebound, accumulation, etc.).
- Mark support and resistance zones; check momentum indicators (RSI, MACD, volume).
- Evaluate the current price position relative to historical highs/lows.

## 5. Synthesis and Conclusion
- Combine the factors above to identify the main catalyst behind the rally.
- Give a view on sustainability, risks, and key points to watch for holders or fresh buyers.
- Note whether there is still no major macro risk or whether any abnormal condition could reverse the trend.
