## Skill: Breadth Divergence Framework

### Definition
"Green skin, red heart" = VN-Index rises driven by a few large pillars (especially VIC/Vingroup which accounts for ~16% of the index) while most stocks fall. International name: narrow breadth rally / market breadth divergence.

### 4 Core Measurement Indicators
1. **Breadth (A/D)**: More stocks down > 1.5x stocks up while the index is still green = narrow breadth
2. **VIC Contribution %**: VIC contribution / total index points > 50% continuously = extreme dependency
3. **Sector Breadth**: Only 1-2 sectors green / 14+ sectors red = extreme polarization
4. **Mid-cap Volume**: Mid-cap liquidity gradually declining = money flowing out of mid/small caps

### 7 Leading Indicators Scorecard (upgraded)
Use when you want early detection of money concentrating into leaders/VN30 before breadth deterioration becomes obvious. Score each item 0-10, then average:
1. **NN Flow VN30**: Net buying ≥3 consecutive sessions
2. **ETF Creation / fund issuance**: Signals of new CCQ/fund creation increasing
3. **VN30F Basis**: Clearly positive basis, especially > +5 points
4. **Block Trades in blue-chips**: Large trading sessions lasting 2-4 weeks
5. **SBV / short-rate backdrop**: O/N not too tight, system liquidity ample
6. **Smart-money close behavior**: ATC stronger than ATO, money entering leaders at session close
7. **Catalyst timeline**: Major events like FTSE/MSCI/earnings approaching

Score interpretation:
- **>=7/10**: Strong early signal, may prepare for pillar explosion phase
- **4-7/10**: Mixed, patience needed to wait for confirmation
- **<4/10**: High false signal probability, prioritize defense

### 5-Phase Cycle of Narrow-Breadth Rally
1. **Accumulation**: Pillars build base, volume dries up then gradually rises, breadth not yet clearly bad
2. **Early Signals**: VN30F basis positive, ETF/NN flow improves, mid-cap volume weakens gradually
3. **Breakout**: Pillars pull hard, index breaks resistance, divergence starts to show
4. **FOMO / Peak Divergence**: Media excited, retail chases pillars, breadth at its worst
5. **Endgame**: Either rotation to mid-cap, or correction across the entire market

### 2 Endgame Scenarios
A. **ROTATION** (positive): VIC/pillars sideways + A/D improves + mid-cap volume rises → money spreads to mid-cap
B. **CORRECTION** (negative): VIC/pillars hit by large-volume selling + entire market in red

### Actionable Checklist
- "Green skin, red heart" > 3 sessions + pillars still strong → HOLD pillars with trailing SL, do NOT buy mid-cap, do NOT chase price
- If already in phase 2 and scorecard >=7/10 → may prepare position in beneficiary proxies (VN30/pillars/ETF) instead of waiting for full breakout
- Phase 3-4: prioritize position management, do not open FOMO buys in pillars
- VIC/pillars weakening (large-volume selling) → Reduce exposure, increase cash, tighten SL
- VN-Index sideways + breadth improving (A/D rising) + mid-cap volume recovering → ROTATION → selective mid-cap entry = BEST TIME to buy
- VN-Index down + breadth declining → DEFEND — cash is king
- Divergence persisting >5 sessions without breadth improvement → lower upside expectations, increase vigilance for false breakout in pillar group

### Applied to Routine
- Mid-session / End-of-session routine: check breadth (A/D), sector breadth, VIC contribution
- If detected >3 consecutive sessions → flag WARNING
- Track A/D line trend across sessions to detect rotation early

### VN Statistics
- 2025: Vingroup contributed 51% of VN-Index gains, only 19% of stocks outperformed the index
- Pattern: 3-5 consecutive sessions of "green skin, red heart" = extremely important warning threshold
- International comparison: similar to S&P 500 "Magnificent 7" pattern of 2023-2024

### Principles
- Do not use margin when breadth is narrow
- Always set SL
- Wait for rotation confirmation (2-3 sessions of improving breadth) before entering mid-cap
