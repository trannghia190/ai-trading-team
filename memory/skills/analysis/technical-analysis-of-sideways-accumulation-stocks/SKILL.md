# Technical Analysis Workflow for Sideways / Accumulating Stocks

## 1. Identify the Sideways / Accumulation State
- Check the chart: price should move within a relatively tight range for at least 2 weeks, with no clear uptrend or downtrend yet.
- Bollinger Bands: clearly tightening, signaling that a stronger move may be approaching.
- Volume: fluctuating around average or gradually declining, with no major abnormal spike sessions.

## 2. Evaluate Key Support and Resistance Zones
- Identify the strong support zone (price tests it multiple times without breaking down).
- Identify the nearest resistance zone (price gets rejected there multiple times and has not broken out yet).
- Mark these levels as reference points for trading decisions.

## 3. Analyze Momentum Indicators
- RSI: moving around 50, with no strong divergence.
- MACD: below or close to the signal line, with trend confirmation still missing.
- ADX: below 20, confirming weak trend strength.

## 4. Identify the Breakout Signal
- Only deploy capital / wait to buy when price breaks resistance with strong volume expansion (>1.5× MA20).
- Prefer breakout sessions with a wide real body candle closing above resistance.
- If price breaks below strong support, consider cutting the position or standing aside.

## 5. Risk Management
- Set stop-loss 2–3% below strong support.
- Do not size aggressively before clear confirmation appears.
- Only increase size when the market confirms a new trend through a successful breakout with strong volume.

## 6. Special Notes
- Recheck macro/sector conditions to avoid being blindsided by unexpected news that could reverse the trend.
- Best suited for investors who prioritize safety or use breakout-swing strategies.
