# Market Regime + Emotion Playbook

## Purpose

This reference standardizes how to turn breadth, sector, foreign-flow, and VN30F data into an actionable market-monitor verdict.

It does not replace `SKILL.md`; it helps avoid two common mistakes:
- turning bullish just because the index is green
- turning too bearish just because of foreign selling while internal breadth is recovering

## Decision Stack

Always read the market through this stack:
1. Index / VN30 / VN30F context
2. Breadth confirmation
3. Sector rotation quality
4. Foreign-flow pressure/support
5. Emotion overlay
6. Shortlist actionability

## Regime Definitions

### Broad risk-on
Signs:
- breadth clearly favors advancers
- many sectors are participating together
- volume and trading value are not concentrated only in a few index movers
- the shortlist has more than 1-2 qualified names

Action bias:
- allow `BUY ON TRIGGER`
- but still do not chase names that are already too extended

### Narrow index-led rally
Signs:
- the index is green, but breadth is not confirming
- a few VN30 names are lifting the index
- mid-caps and most stocks are reacting weakly

Action bias:
- prefer `HOLD / NO ADD` or `WATCH ONLY`
- avoid misreading it as broad risk-on

### Mixed rotational session
Signs:
- breadth is neutral or only mildly skewed
- capital rotates between sectors rather than moving in broad agreement
- there are some pockets of strength, but not many

Action bias:
- stay highly selective
- there may be 0-2 names worth tracking, and there is no need to force a fresh buy

### Risk-off / distribution day
Signs:
- breadth is clearly weak
- many sectors are weakening together
- liquidity expands on the sell side or foreign selling is pressing on the tape

Action bias:
- lean toward `WATCH ONLY`, `HOLD`, and `AVOID`
- if you mention a reversal, frame it as a watchlist idea rather than front-running it

### Speculative froth without breadth support
Signs:
- many names or a single theme are heating up and narratives are everywhere
- breadth or base quality does not support the excitement proportionally
- action reward/risk is worsening even while market emotion is high

Action bias:
- no-chase discipline is the priority
- if already held, prefer `HOLD / NO ADD`

## Emotion Overlay Definitions

### cold
- many names are near lows and few people want to buy fresh
- breadth is weak and the tape feels heavy
- do not use this state to auto-bottom-fish

### stable
- balanced, without emotional extremes
- better for reading rotation than explosive momentum

### warming
- breadth is improving
- leadership is becoming clearer
- good for building a watchlist and waiting for triggers

### crowded
- too many people are watching the same narrative
- strong names are rising faster than the quality of their base
- prioritize warnings that fresh entries are deteriorating

### euphoric
- the index or leaders are being pushed hard and anyone outside the move is prone to FOMO
- but reward/risk for fresh positions is usually no longer attractive

## Sector Lifecycle Lens

For a leading sector, try to assign one of these four states:
- ignition: money flow or catalyst has just appeared and has not spread broadly yet
- expansion: many names in the group are confirming and setups are improving
- divergence: leaders are still holding up, but followers are weakening or sector breadth is deteriorating
- fade: the story is old, price reaction is poor, and money flow is leaving

## Shortlist Interpretation Rules

### BUY ON TRIGGER
Use only when:
- the regime is not hostile
- the stock has a confirming setup or is close to confirmation
- the name is not too crowded

### WATCH ONLY
Use when:
- the story is good but the setup is incomplete
- the sector is right but breadth or foreign flow is not confirming yet
- the market is mixed and needs one more confirming swing

### HOLD / NO ADD
Use when:
- the name is strong but the fresh entry is poor
- leadership is narrow or crowded
- holding the existing position is reasonable, but opening a new one is unattractive

### AVOID / TOO EXTENDED
Use when:
- price has stretched far above the base
- most of the catalyst is already priced in
- crowded or euphoric emotion is too strong relative to setup quality

### 0 focus buy now
This is a valid conclusion, and often the correct one.
Use when:
- breadth is not supportive
- foreign flow is pressing hard
- leadership is too narrow
- no sufficiently attractive names remain after the secondary check

## Reporting Language

Prefer language like:
- "The index is green, but breadth does not confirm it, so this is closer to a narrow rally than broad risk-on."
- "The leading group is in expansion, but many names are already crowded, so hold/no-add is more appropriate."
- "The catalyst is real, but the market is only in a warming phase rather than broad confirmation, so keep it at watch-only."
- "No focus buy now is the right conclusion given narrow leadership and heavy foreign net selling."
