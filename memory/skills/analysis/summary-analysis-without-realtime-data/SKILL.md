# Summary Analysis When Realtime Data Is Unavailable

## Purpose
Use this when realtime price/volume data is unavailable or market data is interrupted, but you still need to assess a stock or sector for a temporary decision or strategic direction.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Quickly summarize the relevant macro and sector backdrop
- Gather the main factors affecting the stock or sector: interest rates, FX, commodity prices, macro policy, demand trends, public-investment disbursement, and similar drivers.
- Determine whether the current environment is broadly supportive, neutral, or unfavorable for the stock or sector.

### 2. Perform a provisional technical analysis
- Explicitly note the absence of realtime price or volume data and warn about the limits of any technical read.
- Use derivatives or broad-market indicators if available, such as VN30F basis or market sentiment proxies.
- Do not confirm a trading action without clear technical evidence.

### 3. Synthesize the Bull case and Bear case
- Build the positive case from still-valid macro and sector catalysts plus favorable factors such as industry growth, commodity trends, or company positioning.
- Assess the main risks, including weak demand, input-cost volatility, and foreign-flow pressure.
- Make clear which argument carries the most weight under incomplete realtime data.

### 4. Recommend an action and define risk controls
- Give a temporary recommendation: wait, monitor, avoid new buying, or only take a small probing position if necessary.
- Emphasize the risk of making decisions under incomplete data.
- Recommend reassessment once realtime data is restored.

## Notes
- Use this workflow only when realtime data is unavailable or interrupted.
- Always warn the user about the risk of acting without confirming data.
- Once realtime data returns, prioritize updating the analysis and adjusting the recommendation.
